Definition

Norman’s Action Cycle is a conceptual model that describes how users interact with a system through a sequence of cognitive and physical stages, from forming a goal to evaluating the result of their actions.

Purpose of the Model

The purpose of Norman’s Action Cycle is to identify where users face difficulties during interaction and to help designers reduce confusion by improving system feedback and control.

Seven Stages of Action

  1. Forming the goal
  2. Forming the intention
  3. Specifying the action
  4. Executing the action
  5. Perceiving the system state
  6. Interpreting the system state
  7. Evaluating the outcome

Gulfs of Interaction

Gulf of Execution

The Gulf of Execution is the gap between the user’s intentions and the actions that the system allows them to perform.

Gulf of Evaluation

The Gulf of Evaluation is the gap between the system’s output and the user’s ability to understand whether their goal has been achieved.

Example: Toggle Switch

A user wants to turn on a light using a toggle switch:

  • Goal: Turn on the light
  • Action: Flip the switch
  • Feedback: Light turns on
  • Evaluation: User confirms success

Importance for Interface Design

Designers should reduce both gulfs by providing visible controls and clear feedback so users can easily understand and control the system.
